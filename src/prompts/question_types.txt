- Supplementary Questions: Ask for additional details or expand the scope based on existing information.
- Hypothetical Questions: Propose hypothetical scenarios based on current facts to explore potential impacts or outcomes.
- Analogical Questions: Use analogies to similar situations to inspire new perspectives.  
- Reverse Questions: Approach the question from the opposite angle to challenge existing assumptions.
- Detailed Questions: Dig deeper into overlooked details within the current facts, focusing on specific issues.  
- Restrictive Questions: Add limitations to the question to clarify the scope of the discussion.  
- Comparative Questions: Compare different phenomena or data to examine similarities, differences, and their impacts.  
- Inferential Questions: Use current information to logically deduce potential future scenarios.
